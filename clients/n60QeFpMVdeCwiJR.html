Where We're Starting From

The honest truth

Damel has real results, a real story, and real qualifications. None of them are showing up online. The content that exists doesn't reflect the person doing the work. That's the gap — and it's also the opportunity.

Current gaps

  • ✗No PT or training content visible — core service is invisible online
  • ✗Nutrition qualification never mentioned or demonstrated
  • ✗No personal story — footballer to PT via ACL injury is a powerful arc not being used
  • ✗Zero client testimonials or before-and-after social proof
  • ✗Virtual Wellness Sessions (4-year programme) has never been promoted
  • ✗Inconsistent posting with no clear content rhythm or strategy
  • ✗Mental health positioning not communicated — huge differentiator going unused
  • ✗No content funnel connecting social presence to bookings or school outreach

Strengths to activate

  • ✓TikTok content previously drove direct client re-engagement — proof it works
  • ✓Semi-pro football background adds credibility and cultural relevance
  • ✓ACL recovery in real time — authentic, relatable, on-brand content waiting to happen
  • ✓PT, nutrition and mental health qualifications — multi-discipline authority
  • ✓4-year Virtual Wellness Sessions community — loyal base to film and feature
  • ✓Former PE teacher — credibility in school and youth wellness conversations
  • ✓Currently in Senegal — unique backdrop for content, disciplined headspace
  • ✓Deeply care-driven — emotional authenticity is the differentiator in a saturated space
02

Social Media Mission Statement

The content mission

To show the world that true health is not a body — it's a life. Through honest storytelling, expert-led content, and real community, DD The PT builds trust with the people who need it most: urban youth, teachers, and those who've been told wellness isn't for them.

Brand voice on social

  • →Real, not polished. No stock photography energy. Phone-filmed, honest, from the gym floor or the park or the kitchen.
  • →Expert, not preachy. Damel knows things. He shares them with calm authority, not lectures.
  • →Community-first, not broadcast. The content invites people in. Comments, Q&As, shared experiences — not one-way posting.
  • →Culturally aware. He speaks to urban youth and communities that mainstream wellness often ignores. That awareness should come through in language, references and representation.
03

Content Pillars

1

Pillar 01 · Personal Story

The Journey: From Pitch to Purpose

Semi-pro footballer. PE teacher. ACL injury. Holistic PT. This arc is the brand. Raw, chapter-by-chapter storytelling about how life, sport, injury and purpose shaped who Damel is and why he does what he does. Mental health destigmatisation for urban youth runs through every chapter.

2

Pillar 02 · Injury Recovery

The Comeback: ACL Recovery in Real Time

Document the ACL recovery journey — physically, mentally and nutritionally. This is live, ongoing content with a natural narrative arc. Weekly or bi-weekly updates showing the reality of rehab: the progress, the setbacks, the mindset work. Authenticity that money can't buy.

3

Pillar 03 · Expert Content

Holistic Health Education

Value-led content drawing on PT, nutrition and mental health qualifications. Quick tips, myth-busting, explainer videos. "The thing your PT never tells you about..." style content that positions Damel as a trusted expert, not just a trainer. Builds authority for school conversations.

4

Pillar 04 · Social Proof

Client Stories & Transformations

Testimonials, before-and-afters, client spotlight interviews and session clips. The community that's quietly attended his Virtual Wellness Sessions for four years — those stories need to be told. Trust is built by showing results, not just claiming them.

5

Pillar 05 · Community & Culture

Wellness for the Culture

Making holistic health feel relevant and accessible for urban youth and communities who've been left out of mainstream wellness. Commentary on mental health stigma, cultural attitudes to fitness, the gap in school wellness provision. Builds the teacher and school niche credibility.

6

Pillar 06 · Behind the Sessions

The Work: PT Sessions & Classes

Clips from actual training sessions, the Virtual Wellness Classes (yoga/Pilates + boot camp), and any school or community workshops. This is the content that was missing entirely. Show the product. Film the work. People need to see what he does before they book it.

Platform Strategy

Instagram Primary · Community Hub
Purpose Brand home. Community building. Client acquisition. Credibility for school outreach.
Formats Reels (primary), Carousels for educational content, Stories for daily life and accountability, Lives for Q&A sessions
Frequency 4-5x per week (3 Reels, 1-2 Carousels). Stories daily where possible.
Tone Warm, expert, community-led. Slightly more polished than TikTok but still real.
Key goal Build a community of 1-5k engaged followers who are potential clients and advocates.
Handle @ddthept — keep consistent across platforms
TikTok Discovery · Reach Engine
Purpose Top-of-funnel reach. New audience discovery. Viral potential for key narrative content.
Formats Short-form video (15-60 sec). Storytelling hooks. Talking to camera. Day-in-the-life. Recovery updates.
Frequency 3-4x per week. Repurpose best Instagram Reels here with TikTok-native captions.
Tone Raw, unfiltered, conversational. TikTok rewards personality over production. Be human.
Key goal Get one video per month to 10k+ views. Drive traffic to Instagram for deeper community.
Watch-out Damel dislikes editing — use CapCut auto-captions. Film short, post fast. Imperfect beats absent.
Facebook School Niche · Community Groups
Purpose Reach teachers, school administrators and older community members. Support grant credibility. Group-building.
Formats Cross-posted Reels, written posts with strong copy, Facebook Groups (Virtual Wellness community), Events for online sessions
Frequency 2-3x per week. Mostly repurposed from Instagram. Minimal extra production required.
Tone Slightly warmer and more community-oriented. Less visual, more conversational in captions.
Key goal Build a Facebook Group for the Virtual Wellness Sessions community. Use as a CIC grant evidence base.
Priority Secondary to IG and TikTok in Phase 1. Activate properly in Phase 2 once rhythm established.
LinkedIn School Contracts · B2B Credibility
Purpose Professional credibility with school decision-makers, HR leads, headteachers and local authority contacts. Grant body visibility.
Formats Thought leadership posts (mental health in schools, teacher wellbeing). Stats-led carousels. Case study posts.
Frequency 1-2x per week. Repurpose educational pillar content with a professional frame.
Tone More formal but still personal. Lead with data and impact. The story still matters — it just wears a suit.
Key goal Be discoverable by headteachers and wellbeing leads searching for school mental health solutions.
Priority Phase 2 activation. Set up profile and post 1x per week from Month 2 onwards.

Editing note — solving the bottleneck

  • →CapCut: Auto-captions in one tap. Film, auto-caption, done. No desktop editing required.
  • →Batch filming: Pick one day a week (Sunday or whenever in Senegal) to film 3-4 videos at once. Post across 4-5 days.
  • →Repurpose ruthlessly: Every Instagram Reel should also post to TikTok and Facebook. One piece of content = three posts.
  • →Stories over pressure: If nothing is ready, post a Story. A 15-second update keeps the account alive. Stories require zero editing.

The one rule that overrides everything

Consistency beats perfection every single time. A slightly imperfect video posted on a Tuesday beats a perfect video that never goes up. The goal in Phase 1 is momentum — not viral moments, not a perfect grid, not production quality. Show up. Film. Post. Repeat.
