Before you start

This workbook is designed to be worked through over 1-2 sittings, not all in one go. Your answers save automatically as you type. Be honest rather than aspirational — the most useful answers are the ones that reflect where you actually are, not where you want to be.

Section 01 · Foundation

Define Your Audience

Who is this actually for? The more specific you are here, the clearer everything else becomes — your content, your product, your events, your messaging. "Everyone" is not an audience.

Question 1.1 ANSWERED

Describe your core person in detail. Not a demographic — a human being.

Think about one real person (real or imagined) who picks up a Starcard in a park. How old are they? What do they do? What do they feel on a Sunday afternoon in London? What are they afraid of socially?

Question 1.2 ANSWERED

Which communities or subcultures does your core audience belong to?

We talked about women of colour, creatives, and people battling social anxiety. Which of these feel most true, and are there others?

Question 1.3 ANSWERED

What is the real problem your audience has that Starcards solves?

Not the surface problem ("they feel lonely"). The deeper one underneath it — the thing they feel but might not say out loud.

Question 1.4 ANSWERED

Knowing who to exclude is just as important as knowing who to include. What type of person would completely miss the point?

Section 02 · Purpose

Define Your Mission

Your mission is not your product description. It's the reason the product exists at all — the change you want to make in the world and the belief system underneath it.

Question 2.1 · The Why ANSWERED

Why does Starcards exist? Not what it is — why it matters.

Start with yourself. Why did you actually make this? What were you feeling when the idea came to you? Be personal before you try to be universal.

Question 2.2 · The How ANSWERED

How does Starcards create the change it promises?

Walk through the mechanism. Someone picks up a Starcard. What happens? What does it do to the dynamic between two strangers? Why does the star shape and the stardust concept matter to that moment?

The object

What does holding a Starcard signal to the world around you?

The concept

What does "we are all made of stardust" mean in the context of a stranger approaching you?

The outcome

What does someone feel 10 minutes after a Starcards interaction that they didn't feel before?

Question 2.3 · Draft mission statement ANSWERED

Try to write your mission in one or two sentences. Don't overthink it — this will get refined.

A strong mission statement completes the sentence: "We exist to..." It should be specific enough that a competitor couldn't steal it word-for-word.

Values (what Starcards will never compromise on)

Question 2.4 · Your manifesto line ANSWERED

If Starcards had one line that summed up the whole belief — the sentence you'd put on a wall — what would it be?

This is not a tagline for selling. It's the thing you believe deeply enough to build a movement around.

Section 04 · People

Community Mission and Profile

The cards are a product. The community is the brand. What does the Starcards community stand for, who belongs to it, and what does it feel like to be part of it?

Question 4.1 ANSWERED

What does someone who belongs to the Starcards community believe?

Not what they buy or do — what they believe. What view of the world do they share that makes them part of this?

Question 4.2 ANSWERED

What does it feel like to be part of the Starcards community?

Describe the feeling, not the features. Think about words like: safe, brave, seen, playful, warm, curious, adventurous...

Question 4.3 ANSWERED

How does the community stay connected between events?

We talked about WhatsApp and Discord as options. What does the digital community look like? What happens in there?

Question 4.4 · Your role ANSWERED

Section 06 · Visibility

Personal Brand and Content

Before the strategy, the platforms, the posting schedule — you need to know what story you're telling and why you're the right person to tell it.

Question 6.1 · Your story ANSWERED

What is your personal story that connects to Starcards?

Moving from Stockholm to London and feeling the absence of community — that IS the brand story. But write it in your own words. The specific moments, the feelings, what made you decide to do something about it.

Question 6.2 · First content ANSWERED

If you had to post something this week, what would it be?

Don't think about strategy yet. What feels most honest and most ready to share right now?

Question 6.3 · What's holding you back ANSWERED

What is the real reason you haven't posted more consistently?

Be honest. This isn't about what sounds good — it's about understanding what actually needs to change for this to happen.
