The key finding

The gender split flips completely between platforms. Instagram is 57% male. Spotify is 56% female. TikTok, based on conversation data, is 65–70% female. This is not a contradiction — it is a map. Each platform is reaching a different version of the same audience, and the campaign strategy needs to treat them differently.

TikTok has delivered three distinct viral moments in this period: a strong November spike (81k day on 13 Nov), a sustained January–February run (consistently 10k–56k daily for nearly 6 weeks), and then the biggest wave of all in late May–June 2026. The June peak is not random — something specific went viral around 31 May/1 June (59–60k views those two days) and it sustained at high levels for 10+ days, with a second spike on 28–29 June (71k and 90k) that then crossed over into the Instagram viral event the same days.

Critical observation — viral but inconsistent

There is a pattern of spike and collapse. September and October dropped to 36–43k total for entire months after the August run. The platform rewards him when he posts consistently (Jan–Feb was a long sustained run) and punishes inactivity hard. The Demon campaign's daily posting requirement is not just a marketing tactic — it is what the algorithm needs to maintain reach.

The Spotify cliff — what happened

There was clearly a song or release in May–June 2024 that drove 34k streams in two months with a peak of 2,763 monthly listeners. Then it collapsed completely — by August 2024 it was back to 1,393 streams and never recovered. There was no social campaign sustaining that spike. The audience found the music once, nothing pulled them back, and they left. This is the exact pattern the Demon campaign is designed to break.

The 100% UK Spotify audience is significant. His listeners are all domestic — which is both a constraint and an advantage for the Demon campaign. Every piece of UK-specific content, every London cultural reference, every carnival clip is speaking directly to the people who could stream. The platform is not globally spread — it is concentrated and targetable.
The Problem

The Critical Gap

Social reach is not converting to streams

In June 2026, TikTok delivered 731,864 video views — his best month ever. Instagram had a viral event reaching 192,401 people in a single day. In the same period, Spotify recorded 194 streams for the entire month of June 2026. The audience is watching but not listening.

  • No link in bio during the viral event. Zero link clicks across the entire June–July Instagram period confirms there was no Spotify CTA active when 192k people reached his profile.
  • No music being promoted on TikTok. The content driving 731k views in June was personality-led, not music-led. Views are not converting because the content is not pointing anywhere.
  • The previous spike (May–Jun 2024) shows the same pattern — 34k streams then nothing. Each time a social wave hits, there is no infrastructure to catch and convert it.
  • The TikTok audience (65–70% female) is the right demographic for the Demon narrative — but they are watching entertainment content, not being directed to music.
  • Instagram's male-majority follower base has nowhere to go — they interact but do not click because there is nothing to click to.
Strategy

What the Data Says to Do

TikTok strategy

  • Daily posting is non-negotiable. Sep–Oct collapse proves it. The algorithm punishes gaps immediately.
  • The female 65–70% audience is exactly who the Demon narrative targets. The content fits the platform perfectly.
  • Transition from pure entertainment to entertainment-plus-music CTA mid-campaign (Day 20 onwards).
  • The June viral window (60k+ views daily for 10+ days) shows what consistent quality content can sustain. That is the benchmark to match for the Demon launch window.

Instagram strategy

  • Add a Spotify link to the bio immediately. Jun 28 will happen again — next time there needs to be somewhere for 192k people to go.
  • The male 57% audience on IG skews 25–34 London-heavy. These are core fans — use IG for deeper content, Stories for the campaign narrative.
  • 3 feed posts per week minimum. Current posting is too sporadic to hold the audience that the viral event brought in.
  • Create a link-in-bio destination before the Demon campaign kicks off. Presave page, Linktree, or direct Spotify.

Spotify strategy

  • The first 48 hours after Demon releases are critical. The May–Jun 2024 spike proves the algorithm will respond to concentrated streaming. Every social post on launch day must drive directly to Spotify.
  • The existing 177 followers are warm — they chose to follow and are 100% UK. On launch day, these people need a direct notification. Make sure the release goes through a distributor that triggers artist follower notifications (DistroKid, Amuse, TuneCore all do this).
  • The 35–44 female Spotify cohort is older than the social audience. Curtains (Summer Walker aesthetic, intimate) will likely perform better with this group than Demon. Sequence matters.
  • Pitch Demon for UK R&B, UK trap and mood-based editorial playlists immediately on release. The 2024 peak suggests playlist adds drove that spike — replicate it intentionally this time.
  • Monthly listener count of 85 is the floor. The ceiling, based on the 2024 data, is at least 2,700+. That gap is closed by consistent releases with social campaigns — exactly what this plan is.

The single most important action before Demon launches

Put a Spotify presave link in the Instagram and TikTok bio today. Not on launch day. Today. The next viral event could happen any time — the Jun 28 spike came with no warning. Every day without a link is a day of potential conversions left on the table.
