Act One · Days 1–10

The Setup — Normal Life, First Cracks

The audience is dropped straight into the middle of something that has already happened. No introduction. No context. They piece it together themselves — which is what makes it feel real.

Act Two · Days 11–22

The Escalation — She's Clearly in His Head

The posting time shifts. The energy changes. Something happened and he's not talking about it directly — but he can't stop posting about it indirectly. Staged drama lands here, spaced to give each moment full impact.

Act Three · Days 23–29

The Climax — Something Has Been Decided

He goes quiet. Returns different. The countdown begins. One black image across all platforms. The audience who has been watching for 29 days knows something is coming.

Day 30 — Demon Drops

The Reveal — Everything Clicks Into Place

The song is released. Everything the audience has been watching for 30 days has its explanation. Every post was the story. The song is the ending.

Post-Launch · Notting Hill Carnival (August)

Party Mode — Demon in the Wild

The campaign is over. The song is out. Now the content shifts entirely — Demon playing at carnival, real reactions, no narrative needed. The song does the work.

Pretty Bandit

@prettybandit.wav  ·  Amaan Choudhury  ·  Singer / Rapper · London

Genre

R&B / UK Trap / Afro-swing. Melodic with lyrical depth. Sits between Summer Walker and Central Cee.

Priority tracks

Demon (dark, R&B/trap) · Curtains (intimate, female audience) · Overdosing (Indian elements, Asian diaspora)

Career stage

Emerging independent. Real creative output, real team. Bottleneck is structure and consistency, not talent.

Content personality

Charismatic, natural, personality-led. Performs better being himself than performing. Best content comes from candid moments.

Strengths

Natural on camera. Likeable, funny, real. Strong team. 16–17 days of footage in the bank.

Watch-outs

Loses momentum after content creation. Needs external accountability. Ideas are not the problem — consistency is.

Brand Identity

Who Pretty Bandit Is

Tone of voice

Honest without being heavy. Funny without trying too hard. Talks like a person, not a brand. The content that works best is when he forgets he's making content.

Visual identity

No clear visual system yet. The 238k and 188k pinneds suggest casual phone-shot aesthetic performs better than produced content. Lean into that for the Demon campaign.

Content world

Dating dynamics. Relationship drama. Gender discourse. Real life moments. Music woven in through story, not promotion.

Artist references

Summer Walker (Curtains energy). Central Cee (anonymous rollout model). Ice Spice (organic TikTok-first rise).

Audience relationship

65–70% female on TikTok. They follow because of his personality. The Demon campaign converts that following into music listeners.

Long-term vision

Independent UK artist with genuine community, not just followers. Three-song rollout proving the model before scaling. Twitch for income diversification.

The Rules

Campaign Non-Negotiables

Never

  • Confirm it is a campaign before Day 30
  • Let any team member hint it is planned online
  • Use the word Demon or song title before Day 26
  • Post content that looks too scripted in Acts 1 and 2
  • Confirm or deny speculation — stay silent or be vague
  • Break character on livestreams or in-person events
  • Skip a TikTok posting day without a pre-filmed fallback

Always

  • Post in first person, from the feeling not the concept
  • Let the comment section tell the story — do not correct it
  • Vary posting times organically
  • Mix normal entertaining content with the narrative
  • Screenshot comment threads daily — post-launch content
  • Have next week's content at least partially filmed by Sunday
  • Send weekly update to Jordan every Sunday without being chased
